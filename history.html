Yemen openly declares war on the terrorist organization al-Qaeda.

14-01-2010

"Unraveling the Complex Threads: Yemen's Persistent Struggle Against Al-Qaeda, Internal Insurgencies, and the Broader Global War on Terror"

The conflict in Yemen involving Al-Qaeda is a continuous armed struggle between the Yemeni government, the United States, and their allies, as they contend with cells affiliated with Al-Qaeda. This conflict is integral to the broader Global War on Terror. Commencing in 2001, the government's efforts to suppress Al-Qaeda cells intensified progressively, culminating on January 14, 2010, when Yemen officially declared open hostilities against Al-Qaeda. Alongside combating Al-Qaeda in multiple provinces, Yemen found itself grappling with a Shia insurgency in the north and militant separatists in the south. The conflict with Al-Qaeda further heightened during the 2011 Yemeni revolution, marked by Jihadists seizing control of the majority of the Abyan Governorate, proclaiming it as an Emirate. A subsequent surge in violence unfolded in early 2012, as militants asserted their influence in the southwest amid intense clashes with government forces. On September 16, 2014, a comprehensive civil war erupted as Houthi fighters seized Sana'a, removing interim President Hadi from power. This event led to the fragmentation of the Yemeni government, creating a divide between the UN-recognized administration of President Hadi and the newly established Supreme Political Council by the Houthis. The full-scale civil conflict resulted in the emergence of Islamist groups such as Al-Qaeda and ISIS, heightened insurgencies by the Houthis, and calls for the separation of South Yemen.

Before hostilities broke out, Yemen faced mounting pressure to address the threat posed by al-Qaeda, as militants based in Yemen were responsible for attacks on its key allies, Saudi Arabia and the United States. Al-Qaeda had a lengthy operational history in Yemen, given that the bin Laden family hailed from Hadhramaut, fostering a deep connection for Osama bin Laden with the country.
